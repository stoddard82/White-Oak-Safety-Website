Frequently asked questions

How long will the 10 hour course take?

The OSHA 10-hour course is designed to take ten hours to complete. Some advanced students may finish the course in less than ten hours and still receive their cards. The majority of students will finish the course in about 11 or 12 hours. The 10 hours it takes to complete does NOT have to be done all at once, either.


How do I renew my OSHA 10 card?

To obtain a replacement 10-hour or 30-hour card, contact your Outreach trainer. A replacement card can only be issued if the class was taken within the last five years and only one replacement card may be issued per student per class. OSHA does not keep records of these classes and cannot provide a replacement card.


How do I get my OSHA 10 card for free?

Passing participants will receive a DOL Construction Outreach course completion wallet card within 2 weeks. In order to receive your DOL Wallet Card you must complete the survey at the end of the course. Includes free OSHA 10 Hour Construction Industry Study Guide!


How hard is the OSHA 10 final exam?

The final exam for the online OSHA 30-Hour course has 100 questions; the online OSHA 10-Hour course has 40 questions. The final exam requires a minimum score of 70% to pass.


Do I need both OSHA 10 and 30?

No, they are voluntary. OSHA recommends outreach courses as an orientation to occupational safety and health for workers. However, some states have enacted laws mandating the training.


What does it mean to be OSHA 10 certified?

The term OSHA 10 refers to the OSHA Ten Hour Training that is part of the OSHA Outreach Training Program. The OSHA Outreach Training Program is a voluntary program of training provided by OSHA authorized trainers. Its purpose is to promote workplace safety and health.


How many questions are on the OSHA 10?

The final exam for the online OSHA 30-Hour course has 100 questions; the online OSHA 10-Hour course has 40 questions. The final exam requires a minimum score of 70% to pass. The OSHA Outreach Program only allows 3 attempts for all checkpoint exams, as well as the final exam.


How long does OSHA 30 actually take?

You are required to take a minimum of four days to complete the OSHA 30-Hour Construction training. However, you have six months from the start date to finish the course.


Do OSHA 30 hour certifications expire?

The card issued by the DOL doesnt expire(besides the maritime card) per OSHA rules, but most companies have their workers take the course every 3-5 years.

Who should take the OSHA 30 hour course?

30-Hour OSHA training is generally recommended for supervisors, site leads or managers who will have any sort of safety responsibility. 30-Hour training not only goes a bit more in-depth on the topics, but also includes a wider breadth of topics.


What happens if you fail OSHA 30?

Do you have to take OSHA 10 before 30?

OSHA does not require anyone to complete the OSHA 10-hour or 30-hour Outreach Training courses. However, some states and even municipalities have made the classes mandatory for certain workers, especially in the construction industry.
